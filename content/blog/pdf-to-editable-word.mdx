# How to Convert a PDF to an Editable Word Document

> **Short answer.** To convert a PDF to an editable Word document, open [PDF to Word](/tools/pdf-to-word), upload your file, and download the DOCX. For scanned PDFs, run [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) first so the text layer exists before conversion.

You received a contract as a PDF and need to edit one clause. Or a colleague sent a report and you want to reuse a table. The conversion preserves text, basic formatting, and tables — you get a DOCX you can open in Word, Google Docs, or LibreOffice.

## What you'll need

- The PDF you want to edit.
- A browser.
- Optional: [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) self-hosted for confidential documents.

## Step-by-step

1. **Check if it's a scan.** Open the PDF, try selecting text with your cursor. If you can't select anything, it's a scan — do step 2 first.
2. **OCR scans.** Run [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) to add a text layer. Pick the right language.
3. **Open the tool.** Go to [PDF to Word](/tools/pdf-to-word).
4. **Upload.** Drop the PDF (or the OCR'd version from step 2).
5. **Download.** Open the DOCX in your editor and make your changes.

## What converts well (and what doesn't)

| Converts well | May need manual fixes |
|---|---|
| Body text and paragraphs | Complex multi-column layouts |
| Simple tables | Nested tables or merged cells |
| Headings and lists | Headers/footers with images |
| Bold/italic/underline | Exact font matching |

The output is a working document, not a pixel-perfect replica. Expect to spend a few minutes on formatting cleanup for complex PDFs.

## Common pitfalls

- **Scanned PDF → empty DOCX.** You skipped OCR. Run [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) first.
- **Garbled text.** The PDF uses an unusual embedded font. Try [PDF to Text](/tools/pdf-to-text) for a plain-text extraction as a fallback.
- **Tables misaligned.** Complex tables sometimes split into separate text blocks. Reformat manually in Word.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it free to convert a PDF to Word?

Yes. [PDF to Word](/tools/pdf-to-word) is free, no signup, no watermark. Self-hosting removes all limits.

### Does it work on scanned documents?

Only after OCR. A scan is just an image — there's no text to convert. Run [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) first, then convert.

### Will the formatting be perfect?

No. PDF and DOCX are fundamentally different formats. Text and basic structure carry over; pixel-perfect layout does not. Budget a few minutes for cleanup.

### Is it safe for confidential contracts?

Public tools see your file. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps it local. For legal documents, self-host.

### Can I convert back to PDF after editing?

Yes. Save from Word as PDF, or use [File to PDF](/tools/file-to-pdf) if you want to use Stirling-PDF for the conversion.

## Wrap-up

Converting a PDF to an editable Word document takes two steps: OCR if it's a scan, then convert. Open [PDF to Word](/tools/pdf-to-word), upload, download, and edit in your preferred word processor.
