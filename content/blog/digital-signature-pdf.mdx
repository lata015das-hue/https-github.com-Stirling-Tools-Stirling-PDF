# How to Add a Digital Signature to a PDF

> **Short answer.** To add a digital signature to a PDF, open [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign), upload your PDF and your signing certificate (.p12 or .pfx), and download the signed copy. The signature is cryptographically verifiable by anyone with a PDF reader.

A digital signature isn't a scanned image of your handwriting — it's a cryptographic proof that the document hasn't been altered since you signed it. Courts, governments, and enterprise workflows increasingly require them.

## What you'll need

- The PDF you want to sign.
- A digital certificate file (.p12 or .pfx) with its password. You get these from a Certificate Authority or your IT department.
- A browser.
- Optional: self-hosted [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) for handling certificates securely.

## Step-by-step

1. **Open the tool.** Go to [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign).
2. **Upload the PDF.** Drop it into the upload area.
3. **Upload your certificate.** Select the .p12 or .pfx file. Enter the certificate password when prompted.
4. **Sign.** Click the sign button.
5. **Download.** The signed PDF downloads. Open it in Adobe Reader or any PDF reader — you'll see a blue ribbon indicating a valid signature.

## Digital signature vs. electronic signature

| Digital signature | Electronic signature |
|---|---|
| Cryptographic proof using a certificate | Any mark indicating consent (typed name, image) |
| Tamper-evident — any change invalidates it | No tamper detection |
| Legally binding in most jurisdictions (eIDAS, ESIGN Act) | Legal weight varies by jurisdiction |
| Requires a certificate from a CA | No certificate needed |

This guide covers digital signatures. If you just want to stamp an image of your handwriting, use [Add Stamp](/tools/add-stamp) instead.

## Common pitfalls

- **Certificate expired.** Check validity before signing. An expired certificate produces an invalid signature.
- **Self-signed certificate.** Readers will show a warning. For legally binding use, get a certificate from a recognized CA.
- **Signing after modifying.** Sign last. Any edit after signing invalidates the signature. Even adding a watermark breaks it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it free to add a digital signature to a PDF?

Yes. [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign) is free. You bring your own certificate — the tool just applies it.

### Where do I get a certificate?

From a Certificate Authority (e.g., GlobalSign, DigiCert, Let's Encrypt for S/MIME). Your company IT may also issue internal certificates.

### Can I verify someone else's signature?

Yes. Use [Validate Signature](/tools/validate-signature) to check if a signed PDF's signature is intact and the certificate is trusted.

### Is my certificate safe with this tool?

Public tools see your certificate and its password — avoid them. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps everything on your machine. For production signing, always self-host.

### Does the signature survive printing?

The cryptographic signature is embedded in the PDF data, not the visual appearance. A printed copy is not digitally signed. The recipient must verify the digital file, not a printout.

## Wrap-up

Adding a digital signature to a PDF requires a certificate and one upload. Open [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign), provide your .p12 and the PDF, download the signed result. Verify with [Validate Signature](/tools/validate-signature) if you receive a signed document from someone else.
