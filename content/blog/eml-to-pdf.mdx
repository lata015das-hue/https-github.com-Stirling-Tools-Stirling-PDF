# How to Convert Email (EML) to PDF

> **Short answer.** To convert an email EML file to PDF, open [Email to PDF](/tools/eml-to-pdf), upload the .eml file, and download the rendered PDF. Headers (From, To, Date, Subject) plus the email body are preserved in a clean, printable layout.

EML files are the universal email export format — every email client can produce them. But sharing or archiving them requires a reader that understands the format. Converting to PDF makes the email universally readable, printable, and tamper-evident when paired with a signature.

## What you'll need

- An .eml file (exported from Outlook, Thunderbird, Apple Mail, or Gmail via "Show Original" → save).
- A browser.
- Optional: self-hosted [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) for confidential correspondence.

## Step-by-step

1. **Export the email.** In most clients: File → Save As → .eml. In Gmail: three dots → "Show original" → "Download original."
2. **Open the tool.** Go to [Email to PDF](/tools/eml-to-pdf).
3. **Upload.** Drop the .eml file.
4. **Convert.** Click the action button.
5. **Download.** The PDF contains headers + body in a clean layout.

## Use cases

- **Legal discovery.** Courts accept PDF more readily than EML. Convert and [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign) for authenticity.
- **HR records.** Archive employee correspondence as searchable PDFs.
- **Client communication.** Share email threads with clients who don't have your email client.
- **Personal archive.** Back up important emails in a format that will be readable in 20 years.

## Common pitfalls

- **Attachments not embedded.** The PDF renders the email body; binary attachments (images, documents) may not be included. Save attachments separately.
- **HTML emails render differently.** Complex HTML newsletters may look different in PDF than in your email client. The conversion does its best but isn't pixel-perfect.
- **Character encoding issues.** Non-Latin emails (Arabic, Chinese) need the .eml to have correct charset headers. If text looks garbled, check the source encoding.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it free to convert EML to PDF?

Yes. [Email to PDF](/tools/eml-to-pdf) is free, no signup, no watermark.

### Does it preserve the email headers?

Yes. From, To, CC, Date, and Subject are rendered at the top of the PDF.

### Can I convert multiple emails into one PDF?

Convert each .eml separately, then [Merge PDFs](/tools/merge-pdf) the results into one document.

### Is it safe for confidential emails?

Public tools see your email content. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps it on your machine. For legal or HR emails, always self-host.

### Can I make the PDF searchable?

If the email is text-based (not a scanned image), the PDF is already searchable. For image-based content within emails, run [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) on the output.
