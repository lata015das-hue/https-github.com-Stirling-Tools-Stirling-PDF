# Compress PDF Without Losing Quality: A Practical Guide

> **Short answer.** To compress PDF without losing quality, start with the **Low** compression level in [Compress PDF](/index.html#/tool/compress-pdf), preview the result, and only step up to **Medium** if the size is still too large. For most documents, that single click cuts the file by 30–60% with no visible difference.

PDFs balloon for predictable reasons: oversized scans, embedded fonts that nobody asked for, fillable forms saved with all their state, and screenshots saved at print resolution when a screen-resolution copy was fine. The fix is not one big compression step — it is a small chain of cheap operations that each shrink one source of bloat. Done well, you can compress PDF without losing quality you would actually notice.

## Why this approach helps you compress PDF without losing quality

Compression is a tradeoff. Lossless compression rearranges bytes; lossy compression throws information away. For text-heavy PDFs, lossless gets you most of the win. For PDFs that are mostly scans, the visual quality you care about is image quality, and the right move is to recompress those images at a sane resolution rather than fight the entire file. That ordering is what lets you compress PDF without losing quality you would actually notice on screen.

## Step-by-step

1. **Open the tool.** Go to [Compress PDF](/index.html#/tool/compress-pdf).
2. **Pick a level.** Start with **Low (best quality)**. Most documents shrink 30–60% at this level.
3. **Compare.** Open the original and the compressed copy side by side at 100% zoom. If you cannot see the difference, ship it.
4. **Step up if needed.** If the file is still too large, try **Medium**. Reserve **High** for when delivery beats fidelity (for example, an email attachment that has to fit under 5 MB).

For scanned PDFs, do one extra step: run [OCR PDF](/index.html#/tool/ocr-pdf) first. OCR turns pixel-text into real text, which often lets the compressor drop the image layer's resolution without touching readability.

## Other things that quietly bloat your PDF

- **Embedded subset fonts.** They add up across many fonts. Stick to two font families when you author.
- **Form state.** Run [Flatten PDF](/index.html#/tool/flatten) before compressing if the file is a filled form.
- **Annotations and revisions.** Use [Remove Annotations](/index.html#/tool/remove-annotations) if reviewers' comments do not need to ship with the final.
- **Hidden blank pages from a bad export.** Use [Remove Blank Pages](/index.html#/tool/remove-blanks).
- **Print-resolution screenshots.** Re-export them at 150 DPI for screen, 300 DPI for print, never higher than the destination needs.

## How to compress PDF without losing quality, in order

1. Flatten forms (if any).
2. OCR scans (if any).
3. Remove annotations and blank pages (if any).
4. Compress at **Low**, then test.
5. If you still need to shrink, compress at **Medium**. Stop before **High** unless you accept a quality drop.

## Frequently asked questions

### How small can I get a PDF without losing quality?

For text-heavy PDFs, expect 30–60% reduction at the **Low** setting in [Compress PDF](/index.html#/tool/compress-pdf) with no visible loss. For image-heavy PDFs, you will hit a wall sooner because the images are the bulk of the file.

### Why is my PDF still huge after compression?

Most of the time it is one of three things: it is mostly scans (run [OCR PDF](/index.html#/tool/ocr-pdf) first), it has many embedded fonts you do not need, or it has a kept form state (run [Flatten PDF](/index.html#/tool/flatten)).

### Is online PDF compression safe?

Public SaaS compressors see your file. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps the file on your own infrastructure. If the document is sensitive, host the [Compress PDF](/index.html#/tool/compress-pdf) tool yourself rather than uploading to a third party.

### Does compression hurt OCR or selectable text?

No, if you OCR first and then compress with **Low** or **Medium**. The text layer is small and is preserved. **High** can re-encode images aggressively enough to harm later OCR runs, so do that step in the right order.

### What is the best compression level?

Start at **Low**. Move to **Medium** only if you must. Treat **High** as a delivery-driven choice rather than a default — it is the level where the tradeoff becomes visible.

## Wrap-up

You can compress PDF without losing quality almost every time, as long as you remove the bloat first and pick the gentlest level that solves your size problem. Open [Compress PDF](/index.html#/tool/compress-pdf), try **Low**, and step up only if you have to.
