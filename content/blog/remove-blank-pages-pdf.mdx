# How to Remove Blank Pages from a PDF

> **Short answer.** To remove blank pages from a PDF, open [Remove Blank Pages](/tools/remove-blanks), upload the file, and download the cleaned version. The tool detects pages with no content and strips them automatically.

Scanned documents often end with blank pages. Book exports add separator pages. Mail merges insert blanks between recipients. Removing them manually page-by-page is tedious — automated detection does it in one click.

## What you'll need

- A PDF with one or more blank pages.
- A browser.
- Optional: self-hosted [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) for privacy.

## Step-by-step

1. **Open the tool.** Go to [Remove Blank Pages](/tools/remove-blanks).
2. **Upload.** Drop the PDF.
3. **Detect and remove.** The tool scans each page for content. Pages below the content threshold are removed.
4. **Download.** The output contains only pages with actual content.

## What counts as "blank"

- Pages with zero text and zero images.
- Pages with only a header/footer (depending on the tool's sensitivity setting).
- Pages that are entirely white (or entirely one color for scanned docs).

Near-blank pages (a single page number, a faint watermark) may or may not be detected depending on the threshold.

## Common pitfalls

- **Page numbers removed.** If a page has only a page number and no other content, it may be detected as blank. Review the output.
- **Colored background ≠ blank.** A page with a colored background but no text/images may not be detected as blank.
- **Wanted some blanks.** If you intentionally have blank separator pages, don't use auto-removal — use [Remove Pages](/tools/remove-pages) to target specific page numbers.

The tool shows which pages were detected as blank in the output summary. Review before accepting.
