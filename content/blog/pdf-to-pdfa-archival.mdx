# How to Convert PDF to PDF/A for Archival

> **Short answer.** To convert a PDF to PDF/A, open [PDF to PDF/A](/tools/pdf-to-pdfa), upload your file, and download the archival-compliant version. PDF/A embeds all fonts and prohibits external dependencies, ensuring the document looks identical 50 years from now.

PDF/A is an ISO-standardized subset of PDF designed for long-term preservation. Courts, government archives, and regulated industries require it because it guarantees the document is self-contained — no broken font links, no external resources, no ambiguity.

## What you'll need

- A PDF you want to archive.
- A browser.
- Optional: self-hosted [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) for confidential archives.

## Step-by-step

1. **Open the tool.** Go to [PDF to PDF/A](/tools/pdf-to-pdfa).
2. **Upload.** Drop your PDF.
3. **Convert.** The tool embeds all fonts, removes external references, and produces a compliant file.
4. **Download.** The output is PDF/A compliant.
5. **Validate** (optional). Use a PDF/A validator to confirm compliance if required by your organization.

## PDF vs. PDF/A — what changes

| Feature | Regular PDF | PDF/A |
|---|---|---|
| External font references | Allowed | Prohibited (all fonts embedded) |
| JavaScript | Allowed | Prohibited |
| Encryption | Allowed | Prohibited |
| Audio/video | Allowed | Prohibited |
| Transparency | Depends on version | Restricted (PDF/A-1) or allowed (PDF/A-2) |
| File size | Smaller (may reference external resources) | Larger (self-contained) |

The content looks identical. The structure is what changes — everything needed to render the document is inside the file.

## Common pitfalls

- **File gets larger.** Embedding all fonts increases size. Normal and expected.
- **Transparency lost (PDF/A-1).** If your PDF uses transparency effects, PDF/A-1 will flatten them. Use PDF/A-2 if your archive accepts it.
- **Encrypted PDFs fail.** Remove encryption with [Remove Password](/tools/unlock-pdf) before converting to PDF/A.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it free to convert to PDF/A?

Yes. [PDF to PDF/A](/tools/pdf-to-pdfa) is free, no signup.

### Which PDF/A version should I use?

PDF/A-1b is the most widely accepted. PDF/A-2b adds transparency support. Ask your archive or regulatory body which they require.

### Does it change how the document looks?

No (in most cases). The visual appearance is preserved. Only internal structure changes.

### Is my file safe?

Public tools see your file. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps it local. For legal archives, self-host.

### Can I convert back from PDF/A to regular PDF?

PDF/A is still a valid PDF. Any PDF reader opens it. There's nothing to "convert back" — you just lose the archival guarantees if you edit it.
