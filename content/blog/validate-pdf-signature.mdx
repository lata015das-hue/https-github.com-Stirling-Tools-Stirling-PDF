# How to Validate a Digital Signature in a PDF

> **Short answer.** To validate a digital signature in a PDF, open [Validate Signature](/tools/validate-signature), upload the signed file, and check the result: valid (document unchanged, certificate trusted), invalid (document modified or certificate expired), or unknown (certificate not in trust chain).

Receiving a signed PDF doesn't mean it's trustworthy. Validation confirms two things: the document hasn't been altered since signing, and the certificate belongs to who it claims.

## What you'll need

- A digitally signed PDF.
- A browser.
- Optional: self-hosted [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) for privacy.

## Step-by-step

1. **Open the tool.** Go to [Validate Signature](/tools/validate-signature).
2. **Upload.** Drop the signed PDF.
3. **Check result.** The tool reports: valid / invalid / unknown certificate.
4. **Act accordingly.** Valid = trust. Invalid = reject or re-request. Unknown = verify the signer's identity through other channels.

## What validation checks

- **Integrity:** has any byte of the document changed since signing? Even adding a bookmark invalidates the signature.
- **Certificate validity:** is the signing certificate expired? Revoked?
- **Trust chain:** is the certificate issued by a recognized Certificate Authority?

## Common pitfalls

- **"Unknown" doesn't mean fake.** A self-signed certificate triggers "unknown" even if the document is legitimate. Verify through other channels.
- **Editing after signing invalidates.** Any modification — even [Add Page Numbers](/tools/add-page-numbers) — breaks the signature. Validate BEFORE making changes.
- **Multiple signatures.** A document can have multiple signatures. Each is validated independently.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it free to validate a PDF signature?

Yes. [Validate Signature](/tools/validate-signature) is free, no signup.

### Does validation execute any code?

No. It reads the signature data mathematically. No scripts are executed.

### What if the signature is invalid?

The document was modified after signing. Request a fresh signed copy from the original signer.

### Is my file safe?

Public tools see your file. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps it local.

### Can I validate and then sign myself?

Yes. Validate first to check the existing signature, then [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign) to add your own signature (the original signature may be invalidated by the structural change of adding a second signature — depends on the implementation).

## Wrap-up

Validating a PDF signature confirms document integrity and signer identity. Open [Validate Signature](/tools/validate-signature), upload, check the result. For signing your own documents, use [Certificate Sign](/tools/cert-sign).
