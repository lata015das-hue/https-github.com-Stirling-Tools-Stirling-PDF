# How to Compress a PDF to 100KB Online

> **Short answer.** To compress a PDF to 100KB online, open [Compress PDF](/tools/compress-pdf), try **Medium** compression first, check the output size, and step up to **High** only if Medium doesn't reach your target. Most text-heavy documents hit 100KB at Medium; image-heavy files may need pre-processing.

Email systems cap attachments at 5–25 MB, but many web forms demand under 100KB or 200KB. That's the scenario where this guide helps: you have a specific size ceiling and need to hit it without destroying readability.

## What you'll need

- The PDF you need to shrink.
- A browser.
- Optional: [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) self-hosted for privacy.

## Step-by-step

1. **Check your current size.** Right-click the file, check properties. Know the gap.
2. **Open the tool.** Go to [Compress PDF](/tools/compress-pdf).
3. **Try Medium first.** Upload and compress at Medium. Check the output size.
4. **Still too large?** Two options: go to High, or pre-process (see below).
5. **Download** when under your target.

## Pre-processing tricks to reach 100KB

If Medium compression alone doesn't hit 100KB, the file probably has heavy images or embedded fonts:

- **Scanned pages?** Run [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) first. OCR often lets the compressor drop image resolution aggressively.
- **Fillable form?** Run [Flatten PDF](/tools/flatten) to discard the form state.
- **Annotations?** Run [Remove Annotations](/tools/remove-annotations).
- **Blank pages?** Run [Remove Blank Pages](/tools/remove-blanks).
- **Only need certain pages?** [Extract Pages](/tools/extract-pages) before compressing.

Each step removes a source of bloat before the compressor runs, making the final file smaller than compression alone could achieve.

## The tradeoff at High compression

High compression re-encodes images aggressively. For text-only documents you won't notice. For documents with diagrams, photos, or charts, you may see blurriness at zoom. Always open the result at 100% and check before sending.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I always get a PDF to exactly 100KB?

No. Compression is lossy — there's a floor below which the content is destroyed. If your 50-page scan is 20 MB, getting it to 100KB is unrealistic without removing pages. A realistic target for a 20-page text document is 100–300KB.

### Is compressing a PDF to 100KB online free?

Yes. [Compress PDF](/tools/compress-pdf) is free, no signup, no watermark. Self-hosting removes any practical limits.

### Will the text still be selectable after compression?

Yes. Compression affects image quality, not text layers. If the text was selectable before, it still is after.

### Is it safe to compress sensitive documents online?

Public tools see your file. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps it on your machine. For anything confidential, self-host.

### What if I need under 50KB?

At that size, you're looking at a 1–3 page text-only document. Strip everything else, flatten, compress at High. If it's still too large, the content itself is the limit.

## Wrap-up

Compressing a PDF to 100KB online is a combination of removing bloat first and compressing second. Open [Compress PDF](/tools/compress-pdf), try Medium, and pre-process with [Flatten PDF](/tools/flatten) or [OCR PDF](/tools/ocr-pdf) if you need to go smaller.
