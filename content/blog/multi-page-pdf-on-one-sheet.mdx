# How to Print Multiple PDF Pages on One Sheet

> **Short answer.** To put multiple PDF pages on one sheet, open [Multi-Page Layout](/tools/multi-page-layout), choose pages per sheet (2, 4, 9, or 16), upload the file, and download the rearranged version ready for printing.

A 40-slide presentation doesn't need 40 sheets of paper. Printing 4 or 9 slides per page saves paper, creates compact handouts, and makes documents easier to carry. The tool rearranges pages into a grid layout before you print.

## What you'll need

- A PDF with multiple pages you want to consolidate.
- A browser.
- Optional: self-hosted [Stirling-PDF](https://github.com/Stirling-Tools/Stirling-PDF) for privacy.

## Step-by-step

1. **Open the tool.** Go to [Multi-Page Layout](/tools/multi-page-layout).
2. **Choose pages per sheet.** 2 for side-by-side, 4 for a 2x2 grid, 9 for a 3x3 grid, 16 for dense handouts.
3. **Upload.** Drop the PDF.
4. **Convert.** Click the action button.
5. **Download.** Each output page contains multiple original pages arranged in a grid.
6. **Print.** Print normally — each sheet of paper now has multiple pages on it.

## How many pages per sheet to choose

| Pages/sheet | Best for |
|---|---|
| 2 | Reading two pages side-by-side (books, articles) |
| 4 | Presentation handouts with space for notes |
| 9 | Dense reference sheets, cheat sheets |
| 16 | Thumbnail overviews, document previews |

## Common pitfalls

- **Text too small.** 9 or 16 pages per sheet makes text tiny. Use 2 or 4 for text-heavy documents.
- **Wrong orientation.** If slides are landscape and you're printing portrait, content may be rotated. Run [Rotate PDF](/tools/rotate-pdf) first if needed.
- **Want to undo.** The original pages are still in the source file. Keep it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it free?

Yes. [Multi-Page Layout](/tools/multi-page-layout) is free, no signup.

### Does it change page content?

No. Pages are shrunk and arranged in a grid. Content is identical, just smaller.

### Can I choose the order (left-to-right vs. top-to-bottom)?

The default is left-to-right, top-to-bottom (reading order). Some tools offer alternative arrangements.

### Is my file safe?

Public tools see your file. Self-hosted Stirling-PDF keeps it local.

### Can I do the reverse — split a multi-up page back into individual pages?

Not automatically. You'd need to [Crop PDF](/tools/crop) each quarter of the page separately.

## Wrap-up

Putting multiple PDF pages on one sheet saves paper and creates compact handouts. Open [Multi-Page Layout](/tools/multi-page-layout), pick your grid size, upload, download the rearranged file. Print it normally.
